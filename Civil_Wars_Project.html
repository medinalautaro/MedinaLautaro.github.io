Impact of Civil Wars and invasions on the
population throughout history

Using data scraping from Wikipedia in Python, data cleanup
and transformation in Excel, and visualization and presentation in Tableau.

Link to interactive visualization

Summary:

This project started as an attempt to properly compare the effects of different civil wars and invasions throughout history, for this it uses a casualty/population ratio. To obtain the information required I scraped from Wikipedia the estimates of the total population of the countries and the deaths produced by each war from the 1500s up to the 2010s using Python. Next, I cleaned the data using Excel, adding new columns for the dates, changing a couple of countries' names from old to modern ones, and getting a death range average for the casualties estimates. Finally, I designed the visualization in Tableau using parameters and calculated fields to display the information on the map chart.

Final thoughts:

Even though the data on casualties and the population is very imprecise for anything previous to 1900 since it relies on calculations and historical accounts that are not always accurate, we can still see the effects of the biggest wars in the countries, and thanks to the visualization, we can have a better sense of what it meant for the local population at the time.
